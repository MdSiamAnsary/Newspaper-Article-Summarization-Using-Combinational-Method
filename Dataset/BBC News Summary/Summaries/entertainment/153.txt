US hip-hop duo OutKast have capped a year of award glory with three prizes at the MTV Europe Music Awards in Rome. R&B singer Usher won best male and best album for Confessions, while UK rock band Muse were named best alternative act and best British artists. Their double CD album Speakerboxxx/The Love Below, which saw Andre 3000 and Big Boi each produce one disc, was hailed as the album of 2003 by many critics. And Anastacia, Britney Spears, Maroon 5 and Ludacris had been on the shortlist for best song. Usher, who performed a duet with Alicia Keys, also beat off competition from Justin Timberlake, Jay-Z, Nelly and Robbie Williams to take the best male crown. Eminem's group, D12, were surprise winners in the best hip-hop category, beating the Beastie Boys, Jay-Z, Kanye West and Nelly.
