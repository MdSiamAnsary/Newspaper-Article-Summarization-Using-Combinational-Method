Wipro, India's third-biggest software firm, has reported a 60% rise in profit, topping market expectations. Wipro offers services such as call centres to foreign clients and has worked for more than half of the companies on the Fortune 500 list. Wipro said demand was strong, allowing it to increase the prices it charged. "On the face of it, the results don't look very exciting," said Apurva Shah, an analyst at ASK-Raymond James. "But the guidance is positive and pricing going up is good news. One problem identified by Wipro was the high turnover of its staff.
