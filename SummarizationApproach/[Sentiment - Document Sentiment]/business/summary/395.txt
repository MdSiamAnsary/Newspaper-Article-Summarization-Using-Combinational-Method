Other US carriers, including United, have sought bankruptcy protection, amid high fuel costs and competition from discount carriers. This latest move to boost business has prompted speculation other firms will be forced to match their fares, hurting revenues in the sector. It is also eliminating a Saturday-night stay requirement on discount fares and will give further reductions to customers opting for non-refundable tickets, booking in advance and online. In October, it reached a crucial agreement with pilots on pay and conditions and it has also issued new shares to staff in return for wage cuts. Airline shares closed lower on the announcement, with Delta, Continental and American Airlines all falling by more than 7%.
