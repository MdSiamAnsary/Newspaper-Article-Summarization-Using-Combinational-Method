Oil prices recovered in Asian trade on Tuesday, after falling in New York on milder winter weather across the US. In London, trading of Brent crude was suspended for a public holiday, but the price fell to $39. With milder temperatures expected to continue in the northern parts of the US over the next few days at least, analysts have said the price of oil may fall further - even if the decline was only temporary. "Weather has been the Achilles' heel of this market," said ABN AMRO analyst John Brady. " Despite a fall of more than $12 a barrel from the record highs reached in late October, the price of crude oil remains almost 30% higher than year-ago levels.
