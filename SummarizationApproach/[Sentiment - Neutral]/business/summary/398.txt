The island state is asking for about $320m from the International Monetary Fund to help pay for relief, he said. According to Mr Mendis, half the IMF support could come from a freeze on debt repayments, which would free up resources immediately. The Paris Club of 19 creditors meets on 12 January to discuss a debt moratorium for the nations hit by the tsunami, which ravaged south and east Asia on 26 December. A full reckoning of the economic cost to Sri Lanka of the tsunami will not be clear for some time to come. One side-effect of the disaster has been that the value of the rupee has risen as foreign funds have flooded into the country.
