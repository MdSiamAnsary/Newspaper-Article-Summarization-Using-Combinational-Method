Flanker Colin Charvis is unlikely to play any part in Wales' final two games of the Six Nations. Sonny Parker is continuing to struggle with a neck injury, but Hal Luscombe should be fit for the Murrayfield trip. Centre Parker has only a "slim chance" of being involved against the Scots on 13 March, so Luscombe's return to fitness after missing the France match with hamstring trouble is a timely boost. Said Wales assistant coach Scott Johnson: "We're positive about Hal and hope he'll be raring to go. " Replacement hooker Robin McBryde remains a doubt after picking up knee ligament damage in Paris last Saturday.
